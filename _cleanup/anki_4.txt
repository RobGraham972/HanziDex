What is the definition of the hyperbolic cosine function, \(\cosh(x)\), in terms of the exponential function?	The hyperbolic cosine is defined as \(\cosh(x) = \frac{1}{2}(e^x + e^{-x})\).
Express the hyperbolic sine function, \(\sinh(x)\), using the exponential function.	The hyperbolic sine is defined as \(\sinh(x) = \frac{1}{2}(e^x - e^{-x})\).
State the fundamental identity relating \(\cosh^2(x)\) and \(\sinh^2(x)\).	For all \(x \in \mathbb{R}\), the identity is \(\cosh^2(x) - \sinh^2(x) = 1\).
What is the addition formula for \(\sinh(x+y)\)?	\(\sinh(x+y) = \sinh(x)\cosh(y) + \cosh(x)\sinh(y)\).
State the addition formula for the hyperbolic cosine function, \(\cosh(x+y)\).	\(\cosh(x+y) = \cosh(x)\cosh(y) + \sinh(x)\sinh(y)\).
What is the derivative of \(\sinh(x)\) with respect to \(x\)?	\(\frac{d}{dx}(\sinh(x)) = \cosh(x)\).
Determine the derivative of \(\cosh(x)\) with respect to \(x\).	\(\frac{d}{dx}(\cosh(x)) = \sinh(x)\).
According to Proposition 2.4, what conditions must a continuous function \(f: (a, b) \to (c, d)\) satisfy to have an inverse function \(g: (c, d) \to (a, b)\)?	The function \(f\) must be strictly increasing or strictly decreasing on the interval \((a, b)\).
On the unit circle, how are the trigonometric functions \(\cos(x)\) and \(\sin(x)\) defined for an angle \(x\) in radians?	For a point \((u, v)\) on the unit circle corresponding to an angle \(x\), \(\cos(x)\) is the horizontal coordinate \(u\), and \(\sin(x)\) is the vertical coordinate \(v\).
What is the trigonometric addition formula for \(\sin(x+y)\)?	\(\sin(x+y) = \sin(x)\cos(y) + \cos(x)\sin(y)\).
State the trigonometric addition formula for \(\cos(x+y)\).	\(\cos(x+y) = \cos(x)\cos(y) - \sin(x)\sin(y)\).
What is the double angle formula for \(\sin(2x)\)?	\(\sin(2x) = 2\sin(x)\cos(x)\).
State the primary double angle formula for \(\cos(2x)\).	\(\cos(2x) = \cos^2(x) - \sin^2(x)\).
Using the Pythagorean identity, express \(\cos(2x)\) solely in terms of \(\cos(x)\).	\(\cos(2x) = 2\cos^2(x) - 1\).
Using the Pythagorean identity, express \(\cos(2x)\) solely in terms of \(\sin(x)\).	\(\cos(2x) = 1 - 2\sin^2(x)\).
